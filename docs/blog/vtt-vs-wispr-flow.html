VTT vs Wispr Flow: which Mac dictation app is better?

By Ihor Herasymovych June 7, 2026 7 min read

VTT and Wispr Flow both let you talk instead of type, anywhere on your Mac. The core difference is philosophy: Wispr Flow is a polished, cloud-first AI dictation product, while VTT is on-device and private by default with optional cloud engines you control. Here’s how to choose.

02Privacy

This is the biggest split. VTT transcribes on-device by default, so your audio doesn’t leave the Mac, and there’s no account. If you opt into a cloud engine, it goes straight to that provider with your key. Wispr Flow is cloud-based by design — its AI features run on its servers, which means convenience and consistency, but your speech is processed off-device under its policies. If you handle confidential material, on-device is the safer structural choice.

03Accuracy & AI editing

Wispr Flow leans hard into AI cleanup — auto-formatting, removing filler words, and adapting tone — which can make raw dictation read like an edited draft. VTT focuses on accurate transcription and giving you engine choice (including large cloud models) rather than opinionated rewriting. If you want the app to rewrite as you speak, Flow’s approach is appealing; if you want a faithful transcript you control, VTT fits better.

04Pricing model

Wispr Flow is a subscription — simple and predictable, but you pay whether you dictate once or constantly. VTT is free to start because on-device costs nothing to run; you only pay your provider’s per-minute rate if you choose a cloud engine. For heavy dictators who can stay mostly on-device, that’s a meaningfully lower running cost.

05Platform fit

Wispr Flow is cross-platform, which is great if you switch between macOS and Windows. VTT is deliberately Mac-only — a native Swift/AppKit app that’s small, fast, and behaves like part of the system. If “feels truly native” matters to you, that focus is the point.

Prefer private by default?

VTT keeps dictation on-device unless you say otherwise — no account, free to start, with cloud engines on your own key when you want them.

06Which should you pick?

  • Choose Wispr Flow if: you want cloud-based AI editing baked in, you’re fine with a subscription and an account, and you need Windows too.
  • Choose VTT if: you want on-device privacy by default, no account, a native Mac app, the freedom to bring your own cloud key, and per-language engine control.
